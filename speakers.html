Siddharth Menon

Siddharth Menon is an independent travelling architect, moving between his building projects in the Indian states of Himachal Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Telangana and Karnataka. His work aims to provide contextual and contemporary design solutions to the challenge of building with locally sourced materials like mud, bamboo, stone and wood, community oriented building techniques and indigenous craftsmen in a swiftly globalizing and homogenizing rural India. The use of architecture as a tool to address the ecological, economic, socio-cultural and political aspects of working in the global south forms an important aspect of his work. Siddharth graduated from Mumbai University, India with a Bachelor’s degree in Architecture in 2011. He has worked as an architect at the Auroville Earth Insitute and with Didi Contractor in Himachal Pradesh. When not on his building sites, he writes, lectures and teaches graduate students of architecture in Mumbai where he currently resides.

Alexie Seller

Chief Operations Officer, Pollinate Energy

Alexie is a keen bean for all things energy, having graduated with first class honours from a degree in Mechanical Engineering and Arts, and worked for one of Australia’s most progressive renewable energy retailers before joining Pollinate. She spent much of her uni years teaching ballet, travelling and volunteering in Latin America, and coordinating projects with ALIV, an Australian based organisation working with asylum seekers, which somehow resulted in teaching Hip Hop on Christmas Island! Alexie has also spent time directing the Engineers Without Borders’ Energy Hub in Sydney, and can often be found cycling and taking in the sights of her new hometown, Melbourne.

Arjun Bolangdy

Head of Operations, Pollinate Energy

A social impact enthusiast, Arjun graduated from the RV College of Engineering, Bangalore in 2012, majoring in Mechanical Engineering. Prior to joining Pollinate, Arjun worked as a business consultant at Schneider Electric for almost three years, where he cultivated a passion to serve the underprivileged. While he isn’t beating himself up over Manchester United’s current form, Arjun enjoys reading philosophy and listening to Led Zeppelin.
